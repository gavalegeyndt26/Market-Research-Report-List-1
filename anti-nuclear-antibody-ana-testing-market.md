<p><p>The Anti-nuclear Antibody (ANA) Testing market comprises several types based on testing methodologies. Indirect immunofluorescence is a gold standard technique that detects ANAs by using fluorescently labeled antibodies, providing qualitative results. Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) offers a more quantitative approach, measuring the concentration of ANAs in a sample. Other market types may include newer techniques like microarray assays or multiplex technologies that enhance sensitivity and specificity, catering to diverse clinical needs in autoimmune disease diagnosis.</p></p>
