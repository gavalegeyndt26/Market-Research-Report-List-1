<p><p>Hydrogen storage vessels are categorized into four main types based on their construction and materials. </p><p>Type I vessels are all-metal, typically made from steel, offering high strength but heavier weight. </p><p>Type II vessels combine metal liners with composite overwraps, providing a balance of weight and cost. </p><p>Type III features a metal liner with a composite wrapper, reducing weight further while maintaining structural integrity. </p><p>Type IV vessels use a polymer liner entirely wrapped in composite materials, offering the lightest option for high-pressure hydrogen storage.</p></p>
